This is a list of Pokémon in the order dictated by the Kitakami regional Pokédex.

The Kitakami Pokédex is one of three regional Pokédexes featured in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet, along with the Paldea Pokédex and the Blueberry Pokédex. It was added to the game in The Teal Mask expansion and contains 200 Pokémon. The Kitakami Pokédex also appears as one of the regional Pokédexes in the mobile version of Pokémon HOME, where it can only be filled with Pokémon that originate from Scarlet and Violet.

The 200 Pokémon in the Pokédex follow this breakdown:

  • 94 are exclusive to Kitakami (7 new and 87 returning Pokémon).
  • 97 also appear in the Paldea region.
  • 5 also later appear in Blueberry Academy (Vullaby, Mandibuzz, Geodude, Graveler, and Golem).
  • 4 appear in all three of the Paldea, Kitakami, and Blueberry Academy regional Pokédexes.

Only Pokémon in the Paldea, Kitakami, and Blueberry Pokédexes, as well as a select few outside of the aforementioned Pokédexes, can be transferred from Pokémon HOME to Pokémon Scarlet and Violet.

Unlike in other games, the Pokédex numbers shown in-game in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet do not have leading zeros. However, the Scarlet and Violet Pokédexes in Pokémon HOME do use leading zeros for Pokédex numbers for consistency.

Completion rewards

Like the Paldea Pokédex, the Kitakami Pokédex has its own set of rewards for filling the Pokédex. These items can be claimed by pressing the X button on the Kitakami Pokédex listing.

Entries Reward

100 Bottle Cap

In addition, upon completing the Kitakami Pokédex, the player can talk to Jacq on Reveler's Road to receive a Glimmering Charm.

Trivia

  • This is the first regional Pokédex to include the Spinarak line without the Ledyba line.
  • This is the only regional Pokédex in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet to not include any Paradox Pokémon.
  • This is the only regional Pokédex in Generation IX to not feature any first partner Pokémon.
  • If White-Striped Basculin is not considered a regional form, this is the only regional Pokédex in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet to not include any regional forms.
  • Like the other mascots in Scarlet and Violet, Ogerpon's image has a unique gold border.
  • Some images in the Kitakami Pokédex reference Pokémon biology:
    • Gyarados's image alludes to the myth of a carp leaping over a waterfall to transform into a dragon, the inspiration behind this Pokémon.
    • Snorlax's image references its well-known habit of blocking bridges in the Kanto and Kalos regions.
    • Nosepass's image emphasizes that it is always oriented to face north.
    • Clefairy and Clefable are shown dancing under the full moon, reflecting their lunar rituals.
    • Carbink's image depicts how their shiny, jewel-like bodies attract Gabite.
    • Some images seem to depict predatory relationships, such as Cramorant with Arrokuda, Starly with Applin, and Appletun with Cutiefly.
