BBdex

This is a list of Pokémon in the order dictated by the Blueberry Academy regional Pokédex.

The Blueberry Pokédex is one of three regional Pokédexes featured in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet, along with the Paldea Pokédex and the Kitakami Pokédex. It was added to the game in The Indigo Disk expansion and contains 243 Pokémon, though only 240 are required to be obtained to complete the Pokédex (Walking Wake, Iron Leaves, and Pecharunt are not required). The Blueberry Pokédex also appears as one of the regional Pokédexes in the mobile version of Pokémon HOME, where it can only be filled with Pokémon that originate from Scarlet and Violet; this version of the Pokédex has only 240 Pokémon, as Walking Wake, Iron Leaves, and Pecharunt are excluded.

The 243 Pokémon in the Pokédex follow this breakdown:

  • 178 are exclusive to the Blueberry Pokédex (the 8 new and 170 returning Pokémon).
  • 56 also appear in the Paldea region.
  • 5 also appear in Kitakami (Vullaby, Mandibuzz, Geodude, Graveler, and Golem).
  • 4 also appear in both Paldea and Kitakami (Tynamo, Eelektrik, Eelektross, and Oricorio).

Only Pokémon in the Paldea, Kitakami, and Blueberry Pokédexes, as well as a select few outside of the aforementioned Pokédexes, can be transferred from Pokémon HOME to Pokémon Scarlet and Violet.

Unlike in other games, the Pokédex numbers shown in-game in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet do not have leading zeros. However, the Scarlet and Violet Pokédexes in Pokémon HOME do use leading zeros for Pokédex numbers for consistency.

Trivia

  • The Blueberry Pokédex is the only Pokédex in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet with Pokémon that can be registered but are not required for its completion (Walking Wake, Iron Leaves, and Pecharunt).
  • Hydrapple appears in the Blueberry Pokédex, despite none of its evolutionary relatives appearing in it. It is the only one of Applin's evolutionary relatives with this distinction.
  • Exeggutor is the only Pokémon in Pokémon Scarlet and Violet to have its original form and regional form appear in the same Pokédex.
  • The order for Iron Crown and Iron Boulder is opposite to that of their respective ancient Paradox Pokémon counterparts, Raging Bolt and Gouging Fire, and to their order in the National Pokédex.
  • Despite occurring in the Area Zero Underdepths, Carbink does not appear in the Paldea Pokédex or the Blueberry Pokédex.
  • Like the other mascots in Scarlet and Violet, Terapagos's image has a unique gold border.
  • Some Pokédex images reference Pokémon's biology:
    • Rivalry between Zangoose and Seviper is depicted in both of their photos.
    • Snover's fascination over humans that is mentioned in some of its Pokédex entries.
    • The similarity between Scizor's claw and Trapinch's mouth is compared.
    • Apparent predatory relationships between Pokémon are shown: Sandile and Trapinch, Crabrawler and Exeggcute, and Fletchinder and Pikipek.
  • Although the Hisuian forms of Braviary, Decidueye, Typhlosion and Samurott do not appear in the Blueberry Pokédex, it is possible to obtain a Terarium-caught one by transferring any of their pre-Evolutions that were caught in the Terarium to Pokémon Legends: Arceus, evolving them there to the said forms, and returning them back to Pokémon Scarlet and Violet, though the player would have to deposit the original form instead for it to register in the Pokédex in Pokémon HOME.
